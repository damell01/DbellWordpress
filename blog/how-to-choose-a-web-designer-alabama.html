How to Choose a Web Designer in Alabama

  1. Home
  2. Blog
  3. Choosing a Web Designer

Published March 2025 • DBell Creations

Choosing a web designer is one of the most important business decisions you'll make. The right designer will build you an asset that generates leads for years to come. The wrong one will deliver something that looks mediocre, loads slowly, and costs you more to fix than it did to build. Here's how to get it right.

1. Review Their Portfolio — And Look Beyond Aesthetics

Every web designer will show you their best work. Look closely at these portfolios, but don't just ask "does it look good?" Ask:

  • Do the sites load quickly? (Open them on your phone and test.)
  • Are they mobile-friendly?
  • Do they have clear calls-to-action and logical navigation?
  • Do they look like real business websites, or do they look like templates with names swapped in?

A beautiful portfolio doesn't mean the sites perform well. Look for a designer who understands conversion, SEO, and business goals — not just visual design.

2. Ask About SEO From the Start

Too many web designers build websites with zero consideration for search engine optimization. They deliver a gorgeous site that Google never sees. Make sure your designer can answer:

  • Will my site be built with proper title tags, meta descriptions, and heading structure?
  • Will you submit a sitemap to Google Search Console?
  • Will my site be mobile-responsive and fast-loading (Core Web Vitals)?
  • Will you include schema markup for local SEO?

If they look confused by these questions, that's a red flag. A good web designer in Alabama understands that your website needs to be findable — not just functional.

3. Clarify What You're Actually Getting

Web design proposals can be confusing. Before signing anything, get clear answers to:

  • Who owns the website when it's done? You should own all content, domain names, and code.
  • Will I be locked into a platform? Some designers build on proprietary platforms that charge monthly fees and lock you in.
  • What does ongoing support look like? What's included and what costs extra?
  • How many revision rounds are included? Unlimited revisions sounds good but often means low-quality initial work.
  • What happens if the project goes over budget? Get a clear fixed-price quote or a detailed hourly estimate.

4. Choose Local When Possible

While you can hire a web designer from anywhere in the world, there are real advantages to working with a local Alabama designer:

  • They understand your market. A designer in Fairhope knows the Baldwin County market in a way that someone in India or even California doesn't.
  • Same time zone and responsive communication. When you need something updated quickly, you need someone available during Alabama business hours.
  • Accountability. A local business has a reputation to protect in your community.
  • Long-term relationship. Your website needs ongoing care — having a local partner makes that easier.

5. Beware of These Red Flags

These are warning signs that a web designer may not be the right fit:

  • No contract. Always work with a written contract that specifies deliverables, timeline, and payment terms.
  • They can't explain what they'll build. If they can't give you a clear description of the technology stack and approach, be cautious.
  • Prices that seem too good to be true. A $200 website will look like a $200 website and perform even worse.
  • No client references or reviews. Ask for references and actually call them.
  • They guarantee #1 Google rankings. No one can ethically promise this.
  • Slow communication during the sales process. If they're slow to respond before you're a client, imagine how responsive they'll be after.

6. Prioritize Business Results Over Aesthetics

At the end of the day, your website is a business tool. The goal isn't to win design awards — it's to bring in customers. The best web designers understand that form follows function: a well-designed site serves business goals first, and looks great while doing it.

Ask any designer you're considering: "How will this website help me get more customers?" If they can't give you a clear, specific answer, look elsewhere.
