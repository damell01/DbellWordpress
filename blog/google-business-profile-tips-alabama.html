Google Business Profile Tips for Alabama Small Businesses

  1. Home
  2. Blog
  3. Google Business Profile

Published March 2025 • DBell Creations

If there's one free marketing tool that every Alabama small business should be maximizing, it's Google Business Profile (formerly Google My Business). It's the primary driver of your visibility on Google Maps and in local search results — and most businesses are only using 20% of its potential.

Why Google Business Profile Matters More Than Ever

When someone searches "plumber near me," "best restaurant Fairhope AL," or "web designer Mobile Alabama," Google displays a local pack — a map with three business listings prominently positioned above the organic search results. This real estate is invaluable. Businesses that appear in the top 3 of the local pack get the vast majority of clicks for those searches.

Your Google Business Profile (GBP) is the primary signal Google uses to rank you in that local pack. A poorly optimized or neglected profile means you're invisible to potential customers who are actively looking for what you offer.

Tip #1: Complete Every Section of Your Profile

Google rewards completeness. Profiles that have all fields filled in consistently rank higher than incomplete ones. Go through your profile and make sure you've filled in:

  • Business name — use your exact legal or commonly used business name (no keyword stuffing)
  • Address and phone number — must match exactly what's on your website
  • Website URL
  • Business hours — including special hours for holidays
  • Primary and secondary categories — choose the most specific category that fits your business
  • Business description — 750 characters, include your primary keyword naturally
  • Services and products with descriptions and prices
  • Attributes — things like "women-owned," "veteran-owned," "LGBTQ+ friendly," "wheelchair accessible," etc.

Tip #2: Choose the Right Business Category

Your primary business category is arguably the most important ranking factor in your Google Business Profile. Google uses it to determine which searches to show your listing for.

Choose the most specific category that accurately describes your core business. For example:

  • Don't just choose "Contractor" — choose "Roofing Contractor" or "General Contractor"
  • Don't just choose "Restaurant" — choose "Seafood Restaurant" or "Barbecue Restaurant"
  • Don't just choose "Marketing Agency" — choose "Digital Marketing Agency" or "Web Design Company"

You can also add secondary categories for additional services you offer.

Tip #3: Add High-Quality Photos Regularly

According to Google, businesses with photos receive 42% more requests for directions and 35% more website click-throughs than businesses without photos. For Alabama businesses, photos of your actual location, team, and work are far more effective than generic stock images.

What to photograph:

  • Your storefront or office exterior
  • Your team at work
  • Completed projects or products
  • Behind-the-scenes of your process
  • Your logo and branding materials

Add new photos at least once a month. Google gives a slight ranking boost to profiles with recent photo activity.

Tip #4: Post Weekly Updates

Most Alabama business owners don't know that Google Business Profile has a posts feature — similar to a social media feed. You can publish updates, offers, events, and new products directly on your profile. These posts appear in your Google listing and signal to Google that you're an active business.

Post ideas:

  • A weekly tip or advice related to your industry
  • A current promotion or special offer
  • An announcement of a new service or product
  • A recently completed project or happy client highlight
  • A local event you're sponsoring or attending

Tip #5: Build and Respond to Reviews

Reviews are the #1 trust signal on your Google Business Profile. A business with 50 four-star reviews will consistently outrank one with 10 five-star reviews for competitive searches.

How to get more reviews:

  • Ask every satisfied customer directly — most will leave a review if asked in person
  • Send a follow-up text or email with a direct link to your Google review page
  • Add a "Leave a Review" button or QR code to your receipts, business cards, or email signature

Equally important: respond to every review. Thank positive reviewers by name and address any concerns in negative reviews professionally. Businesses that respond to reviews rank higher than those that don't.

Tip #6: Use the Q&A Feature

The Questions & Answers section of your Google Business Profile lets anyone ask questions about your business — including you. Proactively add your most frequently asked questions and answer them yourself. This not only improves your profile completeness but also keeps accurate information in front of potential customers.

Common Q&As to add: hours, pricing, parking, service areas, what sets you apart from competitors.

Tip #7: Keep Your Information Accurate and Updated

Holiday hours, address changes, new phone numbers, new services — whenever something changes in your business, update your Google Business Profile immediately. Outdated information frustrates customers and hurts your ranking. Google penalizes profiles with conflicting information across the web.
