5 Signs Your Alabama Business Needs a Website Redesign

  1. Home
  2. Blog
  3. Website Redesign

Published February 2025 • DBell Creations

Your website is often the first impression potential customers have of your business. In Alabama's competitive local market, an outdated or underperforming website isn't just an embarrassment — it's actively losing you business. Here are the five clearest signs that it's time for a website redesign.

Sign #1: Your Website Isn't Mobile-Friendly

More than 60% of web traffic now comes from mobile devices. If your website isn't responsive — meaning it doesn't automatically adjust to fit smartphone and tablet screens — you're delivering a frustrating experience to the majority of your visitors.

Signs your site isn't mobile-friendly:

  • Text is too small to read without zooming in
  • Buttons are hard to tap with a finger
  • The page requires horizontal scrolling
  • Images are cut off or don't display properly

Beyond the user experience, Google penalizes non-mobile sites in its search rankings. If your site isn't mobile-responsive, you're likely invisible on Google for the majority of Alabama searchers.

Sign #2: Your Website Loads Slowly

Studies consistently show that 53% of mobile users abandon a website that takes more than 3 seconds to load. In a world of instant results, a slow website is a customer-repelling machine.

Common causes of slow websites include unoptimized images, outdated code, bloated plugins, and cheap shared hosting. An older website built without performance in mind will almost always be slow.

You can check your website speed for free at PageSpeed Insights (pagespeed.web.dev). A score below 50 is a red flag. A redesign with modern, clean code and properly optimized assets can dramatically improve your speed — and your Google rankings along with it.

Sign #3: Your Website Looks Dated

Web design trends evolve rapidly. A website built in 2015 looks noticeably old today, even if you thought it looked great at the time. Visitors make snap judgments about your professionalism and trustworthiness based on your website's appearance — often in less than 0.05 seconds.

Signs of an outdated design:

  • Cluttered layouts with too much text or too many elements
  • Flash animations or Java applets (these no longer work in modern browsers)
  • Comic Sans, Times New Roman, or other outdated fonts
  • Stock photos that feel generic or low quality
  • A design that looks significantly worse than your competitors' websites

If you're embarrassed to share your own website URL, that's the clearest sign possible that a redesign is overdue.

Sign #4: Your Website Isn't Generating Leads or Sales

Your website should be your best salesperson — working 24/7, qualifying prospects, and driving contact form submissions or phone calls. If visitors come to your site but don't take action, something is broken.

Low conversion rate causes often include:

  • No clear calls-to-action (buttons that say "Contact Us," "Get a Free Quote," etc.)
  • A confusing navigation structure that buries important information
  • Contact forms that are broken, hidden, or require too many fields
  • Lack of social proof — no testimonials, reviews, or case studies
  • Messaging that doesn't clearly explain what you do and why a visitor should choose you

A strategic redesign doesn't just make your site look better — it redesigns the user journey to guide visitors toward becoming customers.

Sign #5: Your Website Is Hard to Update

If updating your website feels like a major project — if you have to call a developer to change a phone number, update your hours, or add a new service — your website is working against you. A modern website should give you a simple content management system (CMS) or should be built in a way that makes routine updates quick and straightforward.

Businesses that can't easily update their websites often end up with outdated information — wrong hours, discontinued services, or stale content — which damages both user trust and SEO rankings.

How Much Does a Website Redesign Cost in Alabama?

The cost of a professional website redesign in Alabama typically ranges from $1,500 for a simple refresh to $8,000+ for a full custom rebuild with advanced features. The investment pays for itself quickly when your redesigned site starts generating more leads and converting more visitors.

Quick Self-Assessment

Answer "yes" to any of these? It's time to redesign:

  • ☐ My website doesn't look good on a smartphone
  • ☐ My website is more than 3–4 years old
  • ☐ I'm embarrassed to share my website URL
  • ☐ My website rarely generates calls, emails, or inquiries
  • ☐ Updating my website requires outside help
  • ☐ My competitors' websites look significantly better than mine
