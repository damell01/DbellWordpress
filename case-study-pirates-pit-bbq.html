Pirates Pit BBQ

Family-Owned BBQ Restaurant — Fairhope, AL

Case Study

Real BBQ. Real Roots. A Website That Shows It.

Pirates Pit BBQ is a family-owned, scratch-made BBQ restaurant in Fairhope, AL — founded by former Fairhope Pirates players with deep ties to the community. Everything is made from scratch, the way BBQ is supposed to be.

They needed a website that matched the personality of the brand: bold, no-nonsense, community-first. A full digital menu, their story, and an easy way for customers to find them and know what to expect before they walk through the door.

The Work

Challenge & Solution

⚠️

The Challenge

  • No digital presence — customers couldn't find them or see the menu before visiting
  • Unique brand story (former Fairhope Pirates players) not being communicated anywhere
  • Restaurant industry is highly visual — food imagery and atmosphere needed to shine
  • Menu needed to be comprehensive, easy to read on phones, and reflect the scratch-made spirit
  • Competing in a market where Google search presence directly drives foot traffic
✅

The Solution

  • Dark, bold design with tan accents and skull identity — instantly recognizable and on-brand
  • Brand story section that highlights the Pirates connection and community roots front and center
  • Full digital menu organized into 6 categories: Platters, Sandwiches, Build Your Own, Wingz, Beverages, and Side Pieces
  • Mobile-optimized layout — easy to browse the menu on a phone in the parking lot
  • Local SEO optimized for "BBQ Fairhope AL," "BBQ restaurant Baldwin County," and related searches

Design Approach

Brand First, Every Detail Intentional

The Pirates Pit brand had a strong personality before the site existed — former Pirates football players, community spirit, real food. The design job was to capture that authenticity and put it on screen.

  • Dark & tan color palette

    Dark backgrounds with tan and warm accents evoke smoke, wood, and pit culture — serious BBQ.

  • Skull identity integration

    The skull logo is featured prominently throughout — a bold visual anchor that's instantly recognizable.

  • Community story front and center

    The Fairhope Pirates connection and family roots are told on the site — not buried in an About page.

  • Restaurant-optimized UX

    Location, hours, and menu accessible within two taps on mobile — built for hungry customers on the go.
